About

The concept of the BB-8 droid originated in the mind of director JJ Abrams and special effects artist Neal Scanlan. They wanted to create a new droid that would be different from the previous characters, but would be sufficiently recognizable and attractive to the audience. The main idea behind the design of the BB-8 was to use a spherical shape for the body of the droid, which allowed it to taxi in all directions, even on uneven surfaces. Lucasfilm studio engineers and artists created a working prototype droid that could roll and taxi while maintaining its characteristic shape. A combination of practical elements and computer graphics was used to create a realistic look and movements of the droid.

A significant contribution to the realization of the droid BB-8 was made by droid manufacturers Sphero, who developed a functioning version of the droid for the public. This made it possible not only to produce working models for the film, but also to present a version of the droid available to buyers that could be controlled using a smartphone or tablet. Thanks to its unique design, friendly character and interesting movement capabilities, the droid BB-8 has become a quickly recognizable symbol of the modern Star Wars universe. He gained popularity among fans of the franchise and became one of the most recognizable characters in the new film trilogy.
